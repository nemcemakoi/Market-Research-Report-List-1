<p><p>The vehicle stamping market consists of two primary types: hot stamping and cold stamping. Hot stamping involves the application of heat to form metal sheets into desired shapes, enhancing strength and lightweight properties, making it ideal for complex geometries and high-performance applications. Cold stamping, on the other hand, is performed at room temperature, allowing for precise shapes and minimal material wastage. This method is economical for high-volume production and is commonly used for simpler parts, contributing to the automotive industry's efficiency.</p></p>
