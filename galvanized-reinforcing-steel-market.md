<p><p>The Galvanized Reinforcing Steel market comprises two main types: Conventional Hot-dip Galvanized Rebar and Continuous Hot-dip Galvanized Rebar. Conventional Hot-dip Galvanized Rebar is subjected to a dipping process, where steel is coated in molten zinc to enhance corrosion resistance, making it suitable for various construction applications. In contrast, Continuous Hot-dip Galvanized Rebar is produced in a streamlined process, allowing for consistent coating and improved efficiency. Both types serve to extend the lifespan of structures in harsh environments.</p></p>
